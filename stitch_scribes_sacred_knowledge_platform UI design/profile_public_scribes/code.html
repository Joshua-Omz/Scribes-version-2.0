The Silence of the Desert: Finding Quiet in the Noise

Sr. Maria Kostas • Oct 12

When Anthony first ventured into the deep sands, he did not seek to escape the world, but to confront the architecture of his own soul without the scaffolding of society. Today, our scaffolding is made of notifications and endless feeds. How do we build an inner desert while remaining physically present in a connected city?

Fasting in a Distracted World

Sr. Maria Kostas • Sep 28

We often consider fasting purely in terms of physical nourishment. Yet, the earliest monastic texts speak of 'nepsis'—a state of wakeful sobriety. To fast from digital input is perhaps the most difficult mortification of the modern era, requiring a deliberate turning away from the synthetic light back to the natural shadows of human limitation.
